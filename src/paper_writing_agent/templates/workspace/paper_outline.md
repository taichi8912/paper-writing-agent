# Paper outline and evidence map

Map each section's argument to the evidence and citations that support it. Fill it
in before drafting; it keeps sections focused and citation selection deterministic.

## Abstract

- Goal: problem, advance, headline result, implication.
- Headline numbers (from `stats/`): TODO

## Introduction

- Argument beats: problem -> gap -> approach -> contribution.
- Candidate citations: TODO (available from: introduction)

## Results

For each subsection:

- Claim (the heading, stated as a finding): TODO
- Supporting evidence: figure or table + `stats/` entry.
- Statistical test and verdict: TODO
- Candidate citations: TODO

## Discussion

- Direct answer to the research question: TODO
- Comparison to prior work: 3-5 keys, with the contrast for each.
- Mechanism or rationale: TODO
- Limitations: honest list.
- Future directions: concrete next steps.

## Methods

- Subsections in reading order: data, model, training, evaluation, analysis.
- Each: enough detail to reproduce; defer narrative to Results.
