# Paper outline and evidence map (template)

A per-section map from the argument the section must make to the evidence and
citations that support it. Fill it in before drafting; it keeps each section
focused and makes citation selection deterministic.

## Abstract

- Goal: state the problem, the advance, the headline result, and the implication.
- Headline numbers (from the statistics store): <...>

## Introduction

- Goal: motivate the problem and state the contribution.
- Argument beats: <problem -> gap -> approach -> contribution>
- Candidate citations: <keys> (available from: introduction)

## Results

For each subsection:

- Claim (the heading, stated as a finding): <...>
- Supporting evidence: <figure/table + statistics-store entry>
- Statistical test and verdict: <...>
- Candidate citations: <keys>

## Discussion

- Direct answer to the research question: <...>
- Comparison to prior work: <3-5 keys> with the contrast for each.
- Mechanism / rationale: <...>
- Limitations: <honest list>
- Future directions: <concrete next steps>

## Methods

- Subsections in reading order: <data, model, training, evaluation, analysis>
- Each: enough detail to reproduce; defer narrative to Results.
